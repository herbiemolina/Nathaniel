American Garb

By Thibodeau Trace

Shank of juicing portly American Garb. Take it off! Take it off! Thrust access grease and confused fryer trap faces. My body is covered in the costume of a dying race

When I leave and return it’s as if David became Taft.

One must search for a place in the bountiful groves for love’s measure

Here-with too many places to sit. No wooden capillaries to climb

Time cradled us in a ticking swing, do we refuse to stop it to reach out and walk about -7,000 mile stretches I take atop benches

When age comes prying with blind fortune and commodity, will it roll us under?-or will we stand like Muir in our old age?-darkened from the Forest

Hair of white pine trees withering in autumn’s mist

A gaze that takes a lifetime to receive


Shave the waves of disgrace from your chest, graft the skin from neck and mind
feel your legs pulling tighter.
-let the access evaporate
Build another’s home with it. Fill another’s heart with it

But not in a place

Where they think empty space: a pair of trousers

Where space is more reason for foggy junkyards and material graveyards pushing steam

And when we omit to debasing this our gifted body admits defeat and we’re no longer in our minds except a mind of excess

We accept a seldom cell-you cannot tell me you don’t worry over objects

This mind of excess a constant plunge

Look to the falcon who guides capturing whirlwind

wings sleek, supremely fine

Abundant- the current of nature

starving- the mind of excess
